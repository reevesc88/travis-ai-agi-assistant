# Product name

**Travis**

# Core concept

Travis is an AI-powered business command centre for trades, field-service businesses, small contractors, and growing service companies.

It combines multiple business systems into one dashboard.

The goal is to help a business owner manage calls, emails, calendar bookings, quotes, jobs, invoices, customers, staff, materials, supplier pricing, reports, and admin workflows from one place.

Travis should feel like a practical AI co-worker for small business operators.

It should not feel like a generic chatbot.

It should feel like a modular business operating system.

# Product positioning

Use this positioning:

**Travis is your AI business co-worker. It answers, organises, quotes, schedules, follows up, and keeps the business moving.**

The product should be aimed at:

* Electricians.
* HVAC businesses.
* Plumbers.
* Marine and industrial technicians.
* Small contractors.
* Field-service companies.
* Owner operators.
* Small teams that need admin help but cannot justify full-time office staff.

# Main problem

Small business owners lose time switching between too many systems.

They deal with:

* Missed calls.
* Unanswered emails.
* Messy calendars.
* Manual quoting.
* Job scheduling.
* Supplier price checks.
* Invoice follow-ups.
* Customer records spread across tools.
* Staff coordination.
* Poor visibility of job status and cash flow.

Travis should bring these systems together and use AI agents to reduce admin work.

# Business model

Travis should use a modular subscription model.

Base subscription:

* Core dashboard.
* Customer management.
* Job management.
* Quote tracking.
* Invoice tracking.
* Calendar view.
* Basic AI assistant.
* Basic reports.
* Local or hosted app access depending on deployment.

AI model layer:

* The first version should use OpenRouter free API models where possible.
* The system should support switching AI providers later.
* The AI layer must be abstracted so OpenRouter, OpenAI, Anthropic, local models, and other providers can be added later.
* AI features should degrade gracefully if the free model is unavailable, slow, or rate limited.

Paid add-on services:

* Personal AI receptionist.
* Phone call answering.
* Calendar booking.
* Email inbox assistant.
* SMS follow-up.
* Supplier price monitoring.
* Advanced quoting.
* Document generation.
* Payroll and HR support.
* Advanced reporting.
* Industry-specific modules.
* Multi-user team access.
* Higher automation limits.
* Premium model access.

Make it clear that some services require extra subscriptions because they depend on third-party APIs, phone systems, messaging providers, email providers, storage, or premium AI models.
